Snowball vs. avalanche: what's the difference?

Both methods have you pay minimums on every debt, then throw everything left in your budget at one "target" debt. When the target is paid off, its payment rolls into the next target — the payment "snowballs."

  • Snowball targets the smallest balance first. You get quick wins early, which is powerful motivation, but you may pay more interest overall.
  • Avalanche targets the highest interest rate first. It is mathematically optimal — the least total interest and often the earliest payoff date.

The best method is the one you'll stick with. If the avalanche only saves you a small amount, the motivational boost of the snowball may be worth it. If it saves thousands, that's worth knowing before you choose.
