Why extra payments punch above their weight

Every extra dollar goes straight at the principal, and interest is charged on the principal. So an extra payment doesn't just shrink the balance once — it shrinks every future month's interest charge too. That compounding-in-reverse is why relatively small extra payments can shave years off a loan, especially at high interest rates.

Where should the extra money come from?

Before committing extra money to debt, it's worth confirming that's the best use of it. If your rate is low, investing may come out ahead over the long run — our payoff vs. invest calculator compares both paths directly. And if you have multiple debts, our snowball vs. avalanche calculator will tell you which debt the extra money should target first.
