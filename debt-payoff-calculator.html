How the payoff date is calculated

Each month, interest accrues on your remaining balance at your annual rate divided by 12, then your payment is applied. The calculator repeats this month by month until the balance hits zero. If your payment barely covers the monthly interest, payoff can take decades — and if it doesn't cover interest at all, the balance grows instead of shrinking, which the calculator will warn you about.

Ways to pay it off faster

The two levers are the interest rate and the payment amount. Even a modest extra payment each month can cut years off a high-interest debt — see exactly how much with our extra payment calculator. If you're juggling several debts, the snowball vs. avalanche calculator will find the best payoff order. And if your rate is low, it's worth checking whether investing beats extra payments with the payoff vs. invest calculator.
